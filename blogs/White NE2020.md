## [White, L.V., Sintov, N.D. Health and financial impacts of demand-side response measures differ across sociodemographic groups. Nat Energy 5, 50–60 (2020)](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41560-019-0507-y)

### **Objective:**
- Evaluate the cost and health impacts of TOU (time of use) among vulnerable versus non-vulnerable households that took part in a randomized control TOU pilot 

### **Case:**
- US

### **Methodology:**
- DDD
- Define vulnerability indicators

### **Data Source**

- Survey

### **Findings:**

- The greater cost increases experienced by households vulnerable on the diability and elderly indicators assigned to TOU, relative to their non-vulnerable counterparts

- Households vulnerable on low-income and disability indicators face worse health and comfort outcomes than the outcomes faced by non-vulnerable counterparts
- TOU similarity correlates with worse health outcomes for Hispanic households, relative to their non-vulnerable counterparts
