## [Verbavatz, V., Barthelemy, M. The growth equation of cities. Nature 587, 397–401 (2020)](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-020-2900-x)

### **Objective:**
- Characterize the hierarchical organization of cities, and in particular it quantifies the statistical occurence of large cities

### **Case:**
- US, French, UK and Canadian

### **Methodology:**
- Urban population growth: $\frac{\partial S_i}{\partial t} = \eta_i S_i + \sum_j J_{j \rightarrow i} - J_{i \rightarrow j}$
- Denote: $\sum_j J_{j \rightarrow i} - J_{i \rightarrow j} = I_0 S_i^v \sum_j X_{ij}$
- Stationary distribution

### **Data Source**
- Census bureau

### **Findings:**

- The large fluctuations of Levy noise are able to statistically reproduces such ebbs and flows of cities
