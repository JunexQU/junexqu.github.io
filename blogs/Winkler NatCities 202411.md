## [Winkler, R.L., Clark, J.A.G., Locke, D.H. et al. Unequal access to social, environmental and health amenities in US urban parks. Nat Cities](https://doi.org/10.1038/s44284-024-00153-2)

### **Objective:**
- Examine racial/ethnic differences in access to higher quality parks within cities

### **Case:**
- US

### **Methodology:**
- Regression

### **Data Source**
- Urban parks: Parkserve database
- OSM
- US geological survey
- Census

### **Findings:**

- A significant decline of 8.03% in monthly PM2.5
<center>
<img src="/blogs/review.assets/Winkler NatCities 20241101.png">
</center>

- The poorest 10% contributed marginally 5.4% to HANPP, 4.4% blue-water consumption and less than 3% to the other footprints
- On a per capita basis, the environmental impacts of the top 10% were 4.2 to 77 times that of the bottom 10%
