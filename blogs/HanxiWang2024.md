## [Wang, H. The role of informal ruralization within China’s rapid urbanization. Nat Cities 1, 205–215 (2024)](https://doi.org/10.1038/s44284-024-00038-4)

### **Objective:**
- City-wide occurrence of the Kaihuang policicies and understand their broader distribution and interaction with the urban environment

### **Case:**
- Wuhan, China

### **Methodology:**
- Machine learning
  
### **Data Source: Open**
- Satellite: European Space Agency and Google Earth Pro
- [Socio media post and results](https://figshare.com/projects/Cities_Gone_Wild_Understanding_Informal_Ruralization_Within_China_s_Rapid_Urbanization_2023-12-14_/189495)

### **Findings:**

- The occurrences of kaihuang are not limited to any particular region but it is possible to find instances across the majority of provinces in China

<center>
<img src="/blogs/review.assets/HanxiWang2024001.png">
</center>

- A total of 97.4% of the sites had undergone typical processes of rapid urbanization post-2000, and 66.7% were land that had been vacants since its demolition from rural land, 19.7% were undeveloped land that were used as construction staging areas
- Rapid urbanization is one of the leading causes

<center>
<img src="/blogs/review.assets/HanxiWang2024002.png">
</center>

- Land use after the emergence of kaihuang showed that 24.4% have been redeloped as buildings and 5.9% as parks or green space, while 69.7% of the sites remain undeveloped never returned 
- City is often decribeds as loud/continuous noise, every is expensive, foreign, and a place of struggle
