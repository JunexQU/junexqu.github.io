## [Chen, G., Li, X., Liu, X. et al. Global projections of future urban land expansion under shared socioeconomic pathways. Nat Commun](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-020-14386-x)

### **Objective:**
- Understand of how urban land change will affect other land covers 

### **Case:**
- Global grids

### **Methodology:**
- FLUS model
- CA

### **Data Source**
- Climate scenario: IPCC
- SSPs
- [Land projection 2015-2100](http://www.geosimulation.cn/GlobalSSPsUrbanProduct.html)
- Population: GHSL
- WorldBank and United Nations

### **Findings:**

- SSP5 yields a monotonously increasing trend and the greatest urban land use areas
<center>
<img src="/blogs/review.assets/NatCom/Chen NatCom 20200101.png">
</center>

- Significant sectors include energy generation, resource extaction and manufacturing industries
- On the contraty, the increase of saving rates in China reduced carbon emissions in agricultural and service sectors
<center>
<img src="/blogs/review.assets/NatCom/Chen NatCom 20200102.png">
</center>

- Saving rate increase led to 168.6 Mt of industrial CO2 emission increments, also leadting increase in other countries
- Positive correlation between China's saving rate and CO2 emission
<center>
<img src="/blogs/review.assets/NatCom/Chen NatCom 20200103.png">
</center>

- A 15% decrease in the saving rate of Chinese region can reduce 323 Mt (1.2%) global carbon emission
