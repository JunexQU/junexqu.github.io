## [Ou, Y., Roney, C., Alsalam, J. et al. Deep mitigation of CO2 and non-CO2 greenhouse gases toward 1.5°C and 2°C futures. Nat Commun](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-021-26509-z)

### **Objective:**
- Explore how CO2 and non-CO2 GHG mitigation pathways jointly affect the ultimate level of temperature change

### **Case:**
- Global

### **Methodology:**
- IAM: GCAM

### **Data Source**
- Emission: CEDS
- Non-CO2 mitigation: [EPS](https://www.epa.gov/global-mitigation-non-co2-greenhouse-gases/global-non-co2-greenhouse-gas-emission-projections)

### **Findings:**

- Global CO2 and non-CO2 GHG emissions continue grow throughout the century, driven by increasing population and GDP
<center>
<img src="/blogs/review.assets/NatCom/Ou NatCom 20211001.png">
</center>

- When further accounting for non-CO2 abatement measures, non-CO2 emissions show a decreasing trend
<center>
<img src="/blogs/review.assets/NatCom/Ou NatCom 20211002.png">
</center>

- When additionally including system-wide non-CO2 mitigation measures in comprehensive GHG abatement, industrial and cooling-related HFCs emissions are heavily reduced
