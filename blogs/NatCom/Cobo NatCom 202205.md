## [Cobo, S., Galán-Martín, Á., Tulus, V. et al. Human and planetary health implications of negative emissions technologies. Nat Commun](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-022-30136-7)

### **Objective:**
- Quantify the human health impacts of direct air carbon capture and storage (DACCS) and bioenergy with carbon capture and storage (BACCS) in terms of disability-adjusted life years (DALYs)

### **Case:**
- Global countries

### **Methodology:**
- Life-cycle modelling

### **Data Source**
- Emission

### **Findings:**

- DACCS and BACCS can preserve a substantial number of years of healthy life
