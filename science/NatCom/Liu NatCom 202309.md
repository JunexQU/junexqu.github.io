## [Liu, Y., Cai, W., Lin, X. et al. Nonlinear El Niño impacts on the global economy under climate change. Nat Commun](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-023-41551-9)

### **Objective:**
- Assess ENSO's impact on global economic production using historical climate and economic production data

### **Case:**
- Global countries

### **Methodology:**
- Fixed-effect panel regression
- Counterfactual analysis

### **Data Source**
- Sea surface temperature: HadISST
- Land surface temperature: ECMWF
- GDP: World bank
- Population density: GPW
- IPCC, SSP

### **Findings:**
- ENSO impact persists for 3 years
<center>
<img src="/science/review.assets/NatCom/Liu NatCom 20230901.png">
</center>

- Both extreme EI Nino and La Nina cause damage on economic growth, but the damage is far greater during EI Nino than during La Nina
<center>
<img src="/science/review.assets/NatCom/Liu NatCom 20230902.png">
</center>

- The impact from future ENSO cycles increases in a warming climate, with an exacerbated loss of global ecomomic production from increased ENSO amplitude under greenhouse warming
