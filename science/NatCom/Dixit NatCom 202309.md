## [Dixit, Y., El-Houjeiri, H., Monfort, JC. et al. Carbon intensity of global crude oil trading and market policy implications. Nat Commun](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-023-41701-z)

### **Objective:**
- Present a global high-resolution assessment of crude oil carbon intensity (CI) based on bottom-up engineering-based methods

### **Case:**
- China's provinces

### **Methodology:**
- Life cycle assessment

### **Data Source**
- Oil field, shipping terminal, pipeline, reginery, crude trade and shipping route: Wood Mackenzie, GlobalData, Kpler, S&P Global
- Temperature: NASA MODIS
- Elevation: 

### **Findings:**
- variability in global "well-to-refinery-entrance" carbon intensities at the level of crude trade pathways is significant at 4.2-214.1 kg-CO2-equivalent/ barrel with a volume-weighted average of 50.5 kg-CO2-equivalent/barrel
