## [A. Millard-Ball,M. Reginald,Y. Yusuf,& C. Bian,  Global health and climate benefits from walking and cycling infrastructure, Proc. Natl. Acad. Sci](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2422334122)

### **Objective:**
- Analyse the impacts of urban form, street design, and other factors on active travel mode shares in 11,587 cities from 121 countries

### **Case:**
- Global cities

### **Methodology:**
- Bayesian hierarchical model: impacts of country-level indicators and city-level variables

### **Data Source**
- Google environmental insights explorer

### **Findings:**

- Walking accounts for ~14.3% of trips in cities in the EIE dataset and cycling a further ~2.1%. Public transportation accounts ~23.0%
